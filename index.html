AceManager

You have just seen an ace being served! Ace is a quick winner and eliminates avoidable rallies. To serve an ace, the champion strategizes, plans, pools his or her energy and serves with proper timing, aiming, placement and follows through.

Similarly, Ace-Manager has to strategize, choose proper time, anticipate, execute and follow up for corrective actions. To conserve energy and save time, wasteful activities need to be avoided. This approach will see him or her as a CEO, one day.

Gathering my work experiences over decades, I have recorded podcasts, which could benefit young managers or students of management education, to hone up their managerial skills, eventually developing into effective managers.

Each podcast takes about 6 minutes and there are 52 podcasts in all, grouped under 5 chapters.

Podcasts

  • Ace Introduction
  • 02 Control Focus

    Frauds occur when dishonest employees get an opportunity to make some extra money. Similarly errors can take place when manual interventions are involved in transaction handling. Vulnerable areas where such instances have taken place and possible checks and balances in the form of internal controls are covered in these podcasts.

  • 03 IT Focus

    Over the past 4 / 5 decades, Information Technology has gained a prominent place in business operations and transaction handling. Developments have been phenomenal and in depth IT penetration, taking place, in spite of user resistance in some pockets, is the hallmark of these developments. These milestones, and how IT awareness has become imperative for one and all are covered in this chapter.

  • 04 Bottom Line Focus

    Commercial enterprises are meant to maximize ROI for stakeholders. Managers need to have clarity on how their actions or inactions impact the bottom-line., ie., profit or loss. How activity level and efficiencies / inefficiencies impact the bottom-line are also covered in this chapter, with real instances. Project Cost monitoring insights for a project manager are also included.

  • 05 Planning & Performance Focus

    I have not seen many organizations where planning is really effective. In some organizations, it serves certain limited urposes. Planning has to enable performance monitoring. I have attempted to put together content that brings macro level clarity into long term, medium term and short term plans and ways to make them effective.

  • 06 Process & Governance Focus

    My last chapter in Ace-Manager is on the relevance of processes, which along with controls are like roads with signals, to enable orderly conduct of business. Promoting decisional thinking, place for intangibles and good governance practices with the concepts of enterprise risk management are a part of this chapter.

My Journey

Tulsi Sastri

After qualifying as a Chartered Accountant, in the seventies, started my career in a public sector undertaking engaged in machine tools manufacture.

Moved on to a well known cigarette manufacturing company, which placed great emphasis systems. Had an opportunity to shift to the organization’s EDP (IT as it was called those days), participated in systems development including some innovative applications.

My next organization was an auto ancillary serving automotive and locomotive industries. This organization went through significant IR problems. As its Finance Manager, I had the opportunity to understand the behaviour of costs at different activity levels, and was instrumental in designing a group incentive scheme that brought a turnaround for the plant.

My next organization was a large fertiliser plant which was in the final stages of its construction. Project cost monitoring and Systems for the Operating phase have been good opportunities.

After my stint with the fertiliser company, I had relocated to a metro, to join a process engineering solutions company, followed by a power-cable manufacturing, and after a brief stay with a large steel plant, joined a software services organization. During my 10 year association with this software services entity, it had grown from an employee strength of 3,500 to 27,000. Played the roles of a Controller and Head of Corporate Risk Management here.

The last in my employment journey was with a consulting organization, which also recorded significant growth during my 10 year stint with them. I have been instrumental in introducing Virtual CFO Services as a service offering by this organization, which has become its main say. Moved on to the role of an Advisor.

Feeling stale after every 4 or 5 years in an organization prompted me to make these moves, which were quite beneficial to me in the form of rich experiences. With a desire to share my experiences, published a book, titled, “Translating Operations into Money”, and now I have come up with Ace-Manager podcast series.

You will be able to connect this narrative with many podcasts of Ace-Manager. Thank you for your patient reading and attention.
I like to pursue projects which can make my paren’t field accounting, more effective, with productive deployment of innovations in IT. Shall keep you posted of progress.
